Hello there! M Biehl from meat space here. I wanted to start this off with a handwritten intro, since I'm just not going to take the time to remove the AI-ness from the main readme entirely, and I know for many of you (myself included) when you start to feel the AI voice in a text your eyes glaze over. I've been working professionally almost exclusively with agentic coding for the better part of the last year, and have put a lot of thought into how to get the most out of this technology without burdening yourself and your team with piles of slop. This project is an attempt to leverage agents in a way that doesn't get in the way of human workflows, only adds to them. There are certain tasks that humans are still much better at, and some that agents are clearly the better choice. Designing the architecture of a complex application requires human cognition. But searching through documentation or logs, keeping documentation aligned and up to date, cross referencing huge amounts of data, following a strictly defined workflow... I'm afraid we've lost the race here, and must accept that these tools are simply better than anything we had before.
